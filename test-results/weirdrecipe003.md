Ingredients
Eggs — Fresh Raw · 8 eggs
Corn — Fresh · about 2 cups
Garlic — Fresh · 2 cloves
Butter — Refrigerated · 1 tablespoon
Onion powder — 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper — 1/4 teaspoon
Chicken broth — 1/2 cup
Milk — Refrigerated · 1/2 cup
Cornstarch — 1 tablespoon
Salt — only after tasting, if needed

Recipe adjustments
These substitutions or omissions keep the recipe aligned with My Kitchen:

Garlic powder — omit it. A selected ingredient already provides this flavor identity; omit the duplicate seasoning unless you deliberately want it stronger.
Cooking plan
•
Tonight we are making Eggs, Corn & Garlic Composed Plate. It is planned for about 19 minutes and high energy. We will take it one stage at a time.

•
Before you begin, gather the ingredients and equipment for Eggs & Corn & Garlic.

1
Minutes 0–3: Ingredient Prep:

- Crack Eggs into a separate bowl, check for shell and whisk for scrambled eggs.

Season with Onion powder & Black pepper & Salt.

- Husk Corn when on the cob, or drain canned kernels and break up frozen clumps.

- Prepare Garlic in the form recorded in My Kitchen; mince fresh forms evenly.

- Measure Onion powder & Black pepper & Salt.

Whisk the Chicken broth and Milk together.

In a small cup, stir the Cornstarch with 1 tablespoon cold water until smooth.

2
Minutes 3–4: Add Garlic to hot fat and stir just until fragrant, about 30–60 seconds, before adding wetter ingredients.

Aim for fragrant without dark or bitter edges.

Stop when strong aroma appears before visible burning.

Transfer Garlic to a plate and keep it warm before reusing the skillet.

3
Minutes 4–9: Heat Corn only until tender and hot.

Aim for sweet plump kernels.

Stop when hot and tender without shriveling.

Transfer Corn to a plate and keep it warm before reusing the skillet.

You will need about 3 minutes of attention during this window.

4
Minutes 9–14: Cook Eggs over moderate heat, folding gently as curds form, and remove from heat just before the curds look fully dry.

Aim for tender, moist curds.

Stop when no liquid raw egg remains; cook to 160°F when serving people who need a fully verified endpoint.

5
Minutes 14–17: Add Chicken broth & Milk to the skillet and scrape up the browned flavor.

Bring it to a gentle simmer.

Stir the Cornstarch mixture again, add it gradually, and stir until the sauce lightly coats everything in the skillet.

Taste before adding salt; add only what is needed.

The sauce is ready.

6
Minutes 17–19: Plate Corn & Garlic with the finished sauce.

Add Eggs to the plates and serve immediately.

•
Nicely done. Dinner is ready.
