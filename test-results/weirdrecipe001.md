Cooking plan
•
Tonight we are making Italian Salmon, Broccoli & Pasta Composed Plate. It is planned for about 47 minutes and high energy. We will take it one stage at a time.

•
Before you begin, gather the ingredients and equipment for Salmon & Broccoli & Pasta.

1
Minutes 0–30: Keep Salmon in a leak-proof bag and submerge it in cold tap water.

Allow about 30 minutes per pound, changing the water every 30 minutes; thicker packages may need longer.

Cook Salmon immediately after thawing.

You will need about 4 minutes of attention during this window.

2
Minutes 4–9: While the microwave finishes defrosting the salmon, finish the remaining prep:

- Choose a pot large enough for Pasta and bring the cooking water to a full boil.

- Trim Broccoli and cut it into evenly sized florets, wedges, or halves; include tender stems.

- Measure 1 1/2 cups Tomato sauce.

Measure 1 tablespoon Butter.

Measure 1/2 teaspoon Garlic powder.

Measure 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper.

3
Minutes 15–22: Brown Broccoli briefly, add a small splash of water, cover until almost tender, then uncover and cook off the water.

Aim for tender centers, browned edges, and retained color.

Stop when a fork enters the thickest part with slight resistance and no raw crunch.

Transfer Broccoli to a plate and keep it warm before reusing the skillet.

You will need about 5 minutes of attention during this window.

4
Minutes 25–35: Boil Pasta, stirring early to prevent sticking.

Taste near the package's minimum time, reserve cooking water if useful, and drain when tender.

Aim for tender pasta with the intended bite.

Stop when a tasted piece has no hard center and the desired firmness.

Keep it separate from the protein cooking vessel so each component retains its own texture.

You will need about 5 minutes of attention during this window.

5
Minutes 30–32: Pat dry, remove obvious bones and season.

Season with Garlic powder & Black pepper & Dill & Onion powder.

6
Minutes 32–40: Cook Salmon without moving it until the first side releases, turn carefully, and finish gently.

Aim for moist flakes with a lightly browned surface.

Stop when the thickest portion reaches 145°F and flakes easily.

You will need about 6 minutes of attention during this window.

7
Minutes 40–42: Verify Salmon: The thickest portion reaches 145°F and flakes easily.

8
Minutes 42–45: Add the tomato sauce and seasonings to the browned cooking flavor.

Simmer gently until hot and slightly reduced.

Taste before adding salt.

Stir Chicken broth & Cream of chicken soup & Dill & Onion powder into the skillet as the sauce finishes.

Serve Mustard on top or alongside the finished meal.

The sauce is ready.

9
Minutes 45–47: Plate Pasta & Broccoli with the finished sauce.

Add Salmon to the plates and serve immediately.

•
Nicely done. Dinner is ready.
