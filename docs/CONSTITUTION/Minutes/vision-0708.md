# Stock & Stir Engineering Council
## Vision Change Record (Current Conversation)

## Mission
- Reaffirmed that Stock & Stir exists to help ordinary people, especially those on limited budgets or facing food insecurity, confidently prepare affordable, nourishing meals from pantry-oriented ingredients.
- The free product should fulfill this mission rather than act as a crippled demonstration.

## Product Philosophy
- The planning engine is the primary product asset; presentation layers should follow the engine.
- Build engine intelligence before API or web work.
- Preserve institutional knowledge through Engineering Council Minutes and a living Constitution/HISTORY document.

## Architecture Decisions
- Horizon B (engine intelligence) should precede API stabilization.
- Multi-KO reasoning is the next major milestone.
- The API should expose a stable engine rather than dictate engine design.

## Business Decisions
- Free tier: pantry-first cooking, budget education, cooking confidence.
- Premium begins when SNS remembers the household (inventory, preferences, history, planning).
- Pantry 101 remains free because it fulfills the mission.

## User Experience
- Build confidence, not just recipes.
- Budget-conscious cooking is a first-class experience.

## Marketing
- Focus messaging on affordable cooking:
  - Need a meal for four for about $5?
  - Learn to eat well on a budget.

## Knowledge Base Evolution
- Preserve the future Spice Knowledge Object (internal codename: SpiceSpiceBaby).
- Continue expanding Knowledge Objects through multi-KO reasoning.

## Naming Decisions
- Engineering Council Minutes are the permanent historical record.
- A living Constitution/HISTORY consolidates enduring decisions.

## Roadmap
1. Horizon B engine intelligence.
2. Continue Council Minutes.
3. Build Constitution/HISTORY.
4. Recover older history only if needed.

## Lessons Learned
- Engine development now has higher value than exhaustive historical recovery.
