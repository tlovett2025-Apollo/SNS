# Stock & Stir Vision Change — July 11, 2026

## Change

Stock & Stir is no longer described as a recipe generator or a generic “what is for dinner” application. It is a **Culinary Decision Engine** that rents the judgment of an executive chef to an ordinary household.

## Before

The product was conceived as a pantry-first tool that combined selected proteins, vegetables, foundations, and cuisines into recipes.

## After

The product observes a household kitchen and decides what is possible, desirable, affordable, feasible, and achievable now. It uses stored kitchen context, recognizes opportunities, identifies missing requirements, chooses equipment, adapts to energy, schedules attention, and produces an execution plan.

## Durable Product Definition

> Stock & Stir is a Culinary Decision Engine built on a Cooking Knowledge Base. Knowledge Objects understand ingredients; the Opportunity Engine recognizes possibilities; Equipment and Sauce knowledge determine execution requirements; the Activity Consolidator translates ingredient work into human kitchen work; and the Planner schedules dinner around real equipment and human capacity.

## Primary User Promise

The user should normally provide only:

1. Fresh ingredients available now.
2. Current energy.
3. Current budget.

Stored **My Kitchen** knowledge supplies pantry, freezer, refrigerator, spices, equipment, restrictions, preferences, skill, and household context. Optional cuisine, signature recipe, extra ingredients, or meal shape changes intent and regenerates candidates.

## Business Consequence

There is one complete paid Stock & Stir product rather than a blind free edition and an inventory-aware premium edition. Inventory and household memory are not upgrades; they are the executive chef's eyesight.

Target value proposition:

> Rent an executive chef for approximately 27 cents per day.

Target pricing discussed: $10 per month or $100 per year.

## Architecture Consequence

The permanent loop is:

```text
Kitchen State + User Intent
          ↓
Executive Chef / Opportunity Engine
          ↓
CKB + Knowledge Objects
          ↓
Equipment Manager + Sauce/Flavor Knowledge
          ↓
Have/Need Resolution
          ↓
Activity Consolidation
          ↓
Attention-Aware Planner
          ↓
Schedule-Derived Recipe
```

## Roadmap Consequence

Horizon D proves the loop. The next work is not another redesign of the brain. It is:

- build a point-and-click inventory simulator;
- formalize the UX-to-engine kitchen payload;
- expand the original 250 inventory items into useful culinary knowledge;
- create a structured teaching and validation format;
- extend CKB Studio for batch loading;
- add sauces, techniques, equipment, relationships, and signature recipes through the same architecture.

## Governing Question

Every proposed feature should answer:

> Does this improve the executive chef's understanding of this kitchen or its ability to produce an achievable dinner?
