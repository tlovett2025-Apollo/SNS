Pantry 101

A pantry is a meal-making tool.

The goal is not to own every ingredient. It is to keep a small, visible collection of foods that connect fresh ingredients into meals your household will actually eat.

Build connections, not clutter

A grain or pasta, a few beans, broth, canned tomatoes, cooking fat, and seasonings can support dozens of fresh-food combinations. Start with foods you already recognize and replace what you use.

Record the form you really own

Canned beans, dried beans, cooked beans, and frozen beans do not create the same cooking plan. My Kitchen becomes useful when it remembers the form, amount, and package—not merely the ingredient name.

Let the pantry reduce effort

On low-energy nights, cooked proteins, canned foods, sauces, broths, and familiar sides can shorten active work. On higher-energy nights, the same pantry supports browning, layering, and composed meals.
